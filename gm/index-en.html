The Gospel according to Moses：
A Commentary on Deuteronomy

Publication Objectives

The biblical book of Deuteronomy synthesizes the theology of the Pentateuch (Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers and Deuteronomy) and offers an important starting point for later biblical authors to interpret the history of God’s covenant with Israel. With this three-volume set Inspirata has made a study of the first order on this special book available to the church and the academy. This commentary represents the best of Dr. Block’s lifelong study on Deuteronomy, inviting readers to hear the Gospel (good news) message that Moses delivered in his farewell addresses to Israel on the threshold of the land God had promised to his people. For those who desire clear and sound interpretation that captures Moses’ pastoral heart, or an introduction to scholarship on the historical, literary, theological, and canonical perspectives of Deuteronomy, this commentary is a good place to start.

About the Author

Daniel I. Block, PhD

Festschrift
Festschrift

Festschrift is a borrowed German word for a publication that celebrates and honors in writing a respected scholar. On his seventieth birthday, Dr. Block’s colleagues, friends, and students surprised him with a collection of 25 scholarly essays, in honor of his contributions for the academy and the church to our understanding of the book of Deuteronomy. This Festschrift was published by the prestigious publisher Eisenbrauns under the title For Our Good Always: Studies on the Message and Influence of Deuteronomy in Honor of Daniel I. Block. “For Our Good Always” was taken from Deuteronomy 6:24. With gratitude and a sense of privilege Inspirata hereby publishes his monumental commentary on the Gospel according to Moses.

Daniel I. Block is the Gunther H. Knoedler Professor Emeritus of Old Testament at Wheaton College. He has also taught at Providence Theological Seminary (Manitoba), Bethel Theological Seminary (Minnesota), and the Southern Baptist Theological Seminary (Kentucky). Dr. Block has been actively involved in professional biblical societies and has presented and published more than 120 academic papers on different biblical texts and topics. His major published works include Covenant: The Framework of God’s Grand Plan of Redemption (Baker, 2021); The Triumph of Grace: Literary and Theological Studies in Deuteronomy and Deuteronomic Themes (Cascade, 2017), Ruth: The King is Coming (ZECOT; Zondervan, 2015); For the Glory of God: Recovering a Biblical Theology of Worship (Baker, 2014); Judges, Ruth (NAC; B&H, 1999); The Book of Ezekiel, 2 vols. (NICOT; Eerdmans, 1997, 1998).

Structure of the Commentary

Vol. 1
Introduction to the Book of Deuteronomy
Commentary on Moses’ First and Second Addresses to Israel (Chaps. 1–11)
Vol. 2
Commentary on the first two-thirds of Moses’ Third Address to Israel (Chaps. 12–23)
Vol. 3
Commentary on the remainder of Moses’ Third Address to Israel, Moses’ Fourth Address, and Concluding Poetic and Prose Materials (Chaps. 24–34)
