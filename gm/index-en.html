Recovering the Gospel according to Moses

Commentary on Deuteronomy：The Gospel according to Moses

Purpose of Publishing

This three-volume commentary is the result of more than 20 years of research by the author, Dr Daniel, and his life's work. It provides a complete and in-depth explanation of the richness of Deuteronomy so that biblical researchers around the world can rediscover the gospel in the message of Moses and are touched by the power of God's Word. This commentary focuses on:

  • Clarifying what the text says
  • Showing how the text works
  • Explaining what its message meant to ancient Israelites and why it was Jesus’ favorite book
  • Explaining what its message meant to ancient Israelites and why it was Jesus’ favorite book

Shape and Structure of Book

Volume 1
Introduction to Deuteronomy, Commentary on Moses' first two pastoral addresses
Volume 2
Commentary on the bulk of Moses’ third address
Volume 3
Commentary on the remainder of Moses’ third address and fourth address, Song of YHWH, Moses’ Benediction

Framework of unit

01

Fresh dynamic translation of each literary unit

02

Introduction to the stylistic features and structure of the unit

03

Paragraph by paragraph explanation with tables and illustrations to make it more accessible to readers

04

Theological and canonical reflections on the enduring significance of the biblical text in the context and how it applies to contemporary situations

丹尼尔·布洛克博士
Daniel I. Block, PhD

Daniel I. Block is the Gunther H. Knoedler Professor Emeritus of Old Testament at Wheaton College, and has taught at Providence Theological Seminary, Berthold Theological Seminary, and Southern Baptist Theological Seminary. He is the author of more than 120 academic papers on biblical texts and topics. His major works include Covenant (Baker, 2021), The Gospel according to Moses (Cascade, 2019), The Triumph of Grace (Cascade, 2017), For the Glory of God (Baker, 2014), Deuteronomy (NIVAC, Zondervan, 2012); How I Love Your Torah, O Lord! (Cascade, 2011), Judges, Ruth (TNAC, B&H, 1999), and The Book of Ezekiel (2 volumes, Eerdmans, 1997, 1998).
